TeslaLink User Guide

Master your Tesla's browser with this complete guide to TeslaLink. Learn how to customize your dashboard, manage apps, and use advanced features.

Getting Started: Your First Visit

1. Open the Browser in Your Tesla

Navigate to your Tesla's web browser and enter the following URL: tristankuo.github.io/teslalink

Tip: Bookmark the site for quick access later! You can do this from the browser's address bar.

2. Explore the Default Dashboard

When you first load TeslaLink, you'll see a dashboard pre-populated with popular, region-specific web apps. You can start using these immediately or begin customizing.

Customizing Your Dashboard

Adding a New App

There are two easy ways to add a new app to your dashboard:

  • Manually: Click the "Add App" button. A dialog will appear where you can enter the App Name, URL, and an optional Icon URL.
  • With QR Code: Click the "Scan QR" button. A QR code will appear on your Tesla screen. On your phone, go to the website you want to add, then use your phone's share menu to generate a QR code for that page. Scan the QR code on your Tesla screen with your phone to instantly add the app.

Editing and Deleting Apps

Click the "Edit" toggle at the top of the dashboard. This will reveal "Edit" and "Delete" buttons on each app icon. Click "Edit" to modify an app's details or "Delete" to remove it.

Reordering Apps

While in "Edit" mode, you can rearrange your apps. Simply press and hold an app icon, then drag it to your desired position and release. This allows you to prioritize your most-used apps.

Advanced Features

Fullscreen Mode

For a more immersive experience, especially with video streaming apps, you can use the Fullscreen feature. Click the "Fullscreen" button on the main dashboard. This will open a new, synchronized tab. Launch an app from your main dashboard, and it will open in the fullscreen tab, removing the browser's address bar.

Changing Regions

TeslaLink provides default app lists for various regions. You can switch your region at any time by clicking the region selector (e.g., "USA") at the top of the page. This will update the "Default Apps" list, which you can use to add popular local services to your dashboard.

Light & Dark Themes

TeslaLink automatically detects your Tesla's system theme (Light or Dark) and adjusts its appearance to match. There's no need to change this manually; it just works.
